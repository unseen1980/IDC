Giuseppe Edoardo Arimondi, OSML, OMS, OCI (Savigliano, 26 April 1846 â€“ Adwa, 1 March 1896) was an Italian general, mostly known for his role during the First Italo-Ethiopian War.

 He was one of the few European commanders who gained a victory over the Mahdists before Kitchener's Expedition, soundly defeating them at Agordat in 1893.

 After a long and successfull colonial service, he died in combat at Adwa, and was posthumously awarded the Gold Medal of Military Valor.
